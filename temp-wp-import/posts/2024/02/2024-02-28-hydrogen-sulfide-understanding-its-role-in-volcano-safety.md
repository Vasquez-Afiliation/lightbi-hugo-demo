## Health Effects of Hydrogen Sulfide

Examining the impact of elevated concentrations of hydrogen sulfide on human health reveals a range of severe symptoms and potential risks associated with exposure to this hazardous gas. Volcanic gas, including hydrogen sulfide, poses significant health hazards. High concentrations of hydrogen sulfide can lead to unconsciousness and death in less than an hour, with 500 ppm considered a critical level. Exposure to hydrogen sulfide causes respiratory tract irritation, pulmonary edema, and can paralyze the respiratory center at elevated concentrations. Even at low concentrations, symptoms of hydrogen sulfide exposure include headache, fatigue, dizziness, bronchitis, and lung irritation. It's noteworthy that asthmatic individuals may not readily respond to low levels of hydrogen sulfide, amplifying the risks of exposure-related health effects.

Regulatory guidelines for hydrogen sulfide exposure vary globally, with limits ranging from 0.1 ppm to 100 ppm for different exposure durations and scenarios.
