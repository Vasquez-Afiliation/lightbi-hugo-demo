## Historical Volcanic Eruptions Disrupting Air Travel

How have historical volcanic eruptions disrupted air travel?

Volcanic eruptions have posed significant challenges to air travel due to the ash fallout they produce. The encounters with volcanic ash clouds have resulted in various levels of damage to aircraft, with 26 instances classified as significant to severe. This underscores the risks that aircraft face during eruptions, including engine failure caused by volcanic ash ingestion. Nine documented encounters led to engine failure during flight, highlighting the potential dangers to both aircraft and passengers.

The financial implications of volcanic eruptions on air travel are substantial. For instance, a single eruption incident in Alaska in 1989 incurred repair costs of US$150 million. While there have been no known crashes directly attributed to volcanic ash ingestion, the threat it poses to aircraft safety and infrastructure can't be underestimated.

In addition to the financial repercussions, there are health risks associated with volcanic ash exposure. The abrasive nature of the particles in volcanic ash can cause damage to aircraft surfaces and engines, leading to operational issues. Furthermore, the inhalation of ash particles poses health risks to passengers and crew members.
